# Supplier Payment Boundary Design

## Summary

This document locks the supplier payment settlement boundary and records the core/admin, reversal, bank/treasury, reconciliation, and bank-settlement outcomes. The original boundary step was documentation-only. The implementation later added formal internal Billing/WebAdmin `SupplierPayment` and `SupplierPaymentAllocation` support, added full-payment accounting reversal for posted supplier payments, and added journal-backed bank settlement for posted supplier payments linked to matched reconciliation evidence. It still adds no public WebApi routes, mobile/member contracts, storefront behavior, customer payment/refund flow changes, finance export package format changes, customer invoice archive/download behavior changes, bank API integration, returned-transfer automation, duplicate-payment automation, overpayment/advance policy, or journal editor shortcuts. Bank/treasury and bank settlement decisions are locked in [supplier-payment-reversal-bank-treasury-boundary-design.md](supplier-payment-reversal-bank-treasury-boundary-design.md), [bank-treasury-foundation-design.md](bank-treasury-foundation-design.md), and [supplier-payment-bank-settlement-boundary-design.md](supplier-payment-bank-settlement-boundary-design.md).

The core decision is that future `SupplierPayment` is a formal Billing/Finance settlement flow. It must not reuse customer `Payment`, customer `Refund`, manual journal shortcuts, supplier invoice status text, attachment uploads, notes, or document-only UI as supplier settlement.

## Current Darwin Supplier Payment Findings

- `SupplierInvoice` is the current formal payables source. A payable exists only after a matched and approved supplier invoice is posted through finance posting and stores `PostingJournalEntryId`, `PostedAtUtc`, and `Posted` status.
- `Payment` and `Refund` are current customer/order settlement records. They remain authoritative for customer receivables and must not be reused for supplier settlement.
- `JournalEntry`, `JournalEntryLine`, `FinancePostingService`, `FinancePostingAccountMapping`, `AccountsPayable`, and `CashClearing` exist.
- Supplier invoice posting currently creates liability by debiting `InventoryClearing`, `PurchaseExpense`, and `TaxReceivable` as applicable, then crediting `AccountsPayable`.
- `SupplierPayment` and `SupplierPaymentAllocation` now exist in Billing as the internal/WebAdmin supplier payment core model. They support draft, update, post, cancel-draft, partial allocations to posted supplier invoices, cumulative overpayment guards, AP clearing posting, full-payment reversal, and event/audit evidence.
- Supplier payment reversal is implemented for posted supplier payments only. It creates a balanced `JournalEntryPostingKind.Reversal`, links the original and reversal journals, and reopens payable exposure by excluding reversed payments from paid totals.
- Bank/treasury and bank reconciliation foundations are implemented. Journal-backed bank settlement is implemented as an internal, evidence-backed workflow for posted supplier payments linked to matched zero-difference reconciliation evidence; it is not status-only and cannot rewrite payment, journal, refund, or export history.
- `DocumentRecord`, `ExternalReference`, `BusinessEvent`, and `AuditTrail` are available for payment evidence, target-side ids, lifecycle history, and audit evidence.
- Finance export reads posted journal entries and stored export packages. It must not export supplier payment UI state directly.
- Public WebApi, mobile/member, storefront checkout, customer invoice archive/download, issued invoice snapshots, customer payment/refund flows, and finance export package format remain unchanged.

## Locked Decisions

- `SupplierPayment` is a formal Billing/Finance model and workflow, not the current customer `Payment` and not the current customer `Refund`.
- Supplier payment is allowed only for posted supplier invoices. Draft, matched, approved, voided, or unposted supplier invoices cannot be paid.
- Partial supplier payment is allowed. Completed supplier payment totals cannot exceed open payable unless a future overpayment or advance-payment policy is explicitly designed.
- v1 supplier payment posting debits `AccountsPayable` and credits `CashClearing`. A later bank or treasury foundation can refine clearing accounts without changing the supplier payment boundary.
- Supplier payment settlement is created only through valid finance posting. UI status, supplier invoice approval, attachment upload, note text, or manual journal shortcut cannot settle payable liability.
- Posted supplier payments cannot be voided or deleted. Full-payment reversal is the only implemented correction path after posting.
- Payment evidence uses deterministic `BusinessEvent` and `AuditTrail`.
- Remittance advice, payment confirmation, and related files use `DocumentRecord`.
- Bank, accounting, import, and target-side payment ids use `ExternalReference`.
- Finance export format remains unchanged. Supplier payment and reversal journal entries flow through the existing posted-journal export path.
- v1 is internal/WebAdmin-only. Public WebApi, mobile/member, storefront, customer invoice archive/download, issued invoice snapshots, customer payment/refund flows, and finance export package compatibility remain unchanged.

## Implementation Sequence

1. `Supplier Payment Boundary Design Slice`
   - Outcome: this document. Supplier payment settlement, partial payment, posting, aging, evidence, export, and reversal boundaries are locked before implementation.
2. `Supplier Payment Core Model And Admin Slice`
   - Outcome: completed. `SupplierPayment` and `SupplierPaymentAllocation` exist in Billing with internal handlers, WebAdmin list/detail/create/edit/post/cancel-draft flow, cumulative settlement guards, read-only journal links, and event/audit evidence.
   - The slice adds no public/mobile/storefront exposure, customer payment/refund reuse, manual journal shortcuts, bank API integration, overpayment/advance handling, or reversal flow.
3. `Supplier Payment Reversal And Bank/Treasury Boundary Design`
   - Outcome: completed in [supplier-payment-reversal-bank-treasury-boundary-design.md](supplier-payment-reversal-bank-treasury-boundary-design.md). Posted payment correction, bank account ownership, treasury clearing, bank reconciliation, overpayment/advance, remittance evidence, and finance export boundaries are locked.
4. `Supplier Payment Reversal Core Slice`
   - Outcome: completed. Posted supplier payments can be fully reversed through balanced finance posting with original and reversal journal links, deterministic event/audit evidence, and WebAdmin `Reverse payment` action.
   - The slice adds no direct bank settlement, returned transfer handling, overpayment/advance, bank reconciliation, public/mobile exposure, customer payment/refund reuse, or finance export format change.
5. `Bank/Treasury Foundation Design Slice`
   - Outcome: completed in [bank-treasury-foundation-design.md](bank-treasury-foundation-design.md). `FinancialAccount` remains accounting-owned, `BankAccount` is operational treasury identity, and `CashClearing` remains the AP clearing boundary until journal-backed settlement clears it against a mapped bank Asset account.
6. `Bank/Treasury Foundation Core Model And Admin Slice`
   - Outcome: completed. `BankAccount`, `BankStatementImport`, and `BankStatementLine` exist in Billing with internal WebAdmin coverage, safe metadata handling, statement totals, deterministic line identity, and event/audit evidence.
7. `Bank Reconciliation Core Slice`
   - Outcome: completed. `BankReconciliationMatch` and `BankReconciliationMatchLine` link bank statement evidence to posted or reversed finance facts without mutating supplier payments, customer payments, refunds, journal entries, or finance export history.
8. `Supplier Payment Bank Settlement Boundary Design`
   - Outcome: completed in [supplier-payment-bank-settlement-boundary-design.md](supplier-payment-bank-settlement-boundary-design.md). Bank settlement is limited to posted supplier payments, must be linked to reconciliation evidence, and cannot be status-only or bank API dependent.
9. `Supplier Payment Bank Settlement Core Slice`
   - Outcome: completed. Posted supplier payments can be bank-settled from matched zero-difference reconciliation evidence with a journal-backed clearing entry: debit `CashClearing`, credit mapped bank Asset account. Settlement is full-payment v1, internal/WebAdmin-only, and blocks simple reversal after bank settlement.
10. `Returned Transfer And Duplicate Payment Boundary Design`
   - Outcome: completed in [returned-transfer-duplicate-payment-boundary-design.md](returned-transfer-duplicate-payment-boundary-design.md). Returned/failed transfer correction must be full-settlement v1, evidence-backed by bank statement and reconciliation, journal-owned, and separate from supplier payment history rewrite. Duplicate bank movement is evidence/attention first. Supplier advance/overpayment and bank API adapters stay separate future slices.
11. `Returned Transfer And Duplicate Payment Correction Core Slice`
   - Outcome: implemented. `SupplierPaymentBankCorrection` is the formal internal correction model. Returned-transfer correction posts full-settlement journal-backed correction; duplicate-payment correction remains evidence/attention and cannot auto-post.
12. `Supplier Advance And Overpayment Boundary Design Slice`
   - Outcome: completed in [supplier-advance-overpayment-boundary-design.md](supplier-advance-overpayment-boundary-design.md). Supplier advance must be a formal Asset-backed Billing/Finance concept with explicit posting, allocation, aging, reconciliation, and reversal boundaries. Existing supplier payment overpayment guards remain active until implementation.
13. `Supplier Advance Core Model And Admin Slice`
   - Outcome: completed. `SupplierAdvance` and `SupplierAdvanceApplication` exist in Billing with internal/WebAdmin create/update draft, post, cancel draft, and explicit apply-to-posted-invoice workflows. Advance posting and application are journal-backed and finance-export compatible through posted `JournalEntry` facts.
14. `Supplier Advance Reversal And Bank Correction Hardening`
   - Outcome: completed. Posted unapplied advances and active advance applications can be reversed only through formal finance posting. Reversal preserves source history, opens balances correctly, and does not alter customer payment/refund or finance export contracts.
