# Bank/Treasury Foundation Design

## Summary

This document locks the bank and treasury foundation boundary before Darwin implements returned transfer handling, duplicate payment handling, bank API integration, or supplier advance and overpayment workflows. The core foundation is now implemented for internal bank account master data, bank statement evidence, reconciliation evidence, and journal-backed supplier payment bank settlement. It does not add bank API integration, returned-transfer automation, duplicate-payment automation, public WebApi route, mobile/member contract, storefront behavior, finance export format change, journal editor shortcut, or customer payment/refund flow change.

The core decision is that bank and treasury evidence must be modeled as operational finance facts around posted accounting entries. `FinancialAccount` remains the accounting chart account model. `BankAccount` is the operational bank identity model and must not replace `FinancialAccount`. `CashClearing` remains the v1 AP payment clearing boundary until a controlled supplier payment bank settlement workflow moves cleared supplier payments to a mapped bank Asset account through finance posting.

## Current Darwin Bank/Treasury Findings

- `FinancialAccount` is the accounting account model. It is not an operational bank account, statement source, bank credential owner, or reconciliation identity.
- `CashClearing` is the current clearing role for supplier payment settlement. Supplier payment posting debits `AccountsPayable` and credits `CashClearing`; supplier payment reversal debits `CashClearing` and credits `AccountsPayable`.
- `SupplierPayment` and `SupplierPaymentAllocation` exist in Billing with draft, post, cancel-draft, partial allocation, cumulative overpayment guard, and full-payment reversal support.
- Posted and reversed supplier payments become authoritative through `JournalEntry` and `JournalEntryLine`. `SupplierPayment` stores posting and reversal journal links for idempotency, UI, and audit.
- Darwin now has canonical `BankAccount`, `BankStatementImport`, `BankStatementLine`, `BankReconciliationMatch`, and `BankReconciliationMatchLine` models in Billing for operational bank identity, statement evidence, and reconciliation evidence. Supplier payment bank settlement is implemented as full-payment journal-backed clearing from matched reconciliation evidence. Darwin still does not have a treasury ledger, returned-transfer automation, duplicate-payment workflow, supplier advance, or supplier overpayment model.
- Customer `Payment` and customer `Refund` remain customer/order settlement records and are not reused for supplier settlement, treasury, bank statement import, or reconciliation.
- `ExternalReference`, `DocumentRecord`, `BusinessEvent`, and `AuditTrail` are available for remote ids, bank/remittance evidence, lifecycle history, and audit evidence.
- Finance export reads posted journal entries and stored export packages. It must not export bank metadata, statement rows, payment UI state, or remittance documents directly unless a future posting/export design makes that data part of posted finance evidence.
- Public WebApi, mobile/member, storefront checkout, customer invoice archive/download, issued invoice snapshots, customer payment/refund flows, and finance export package format remain unchanged.

## Locked Decisions

- Future `BankAccount` is an operational treasury/bank identity model and does not replace `FinancialAccount`.
- `FinancialAccount` remains the chart-of-accounts/accounting account model.
- `BankAccount.FinancialAccountId` is optional mapping evidence and does not post accounting or prove bank settlement.
- `BankStatementImport` and `BankStatementLine` are evidence/source facts only; they do not create journal entries, settle payments, reverse payments, match transactions, or alter supplier/customer payment state.
- `CashClearing` remains the current settlement boundary until bank/treasury clearing and reconciliation are implemented through explicit treasury workflows.
- Bank statement entries are source facts and evidence. They are not journal entries, settlements, reversals, or payments by themselves.
- Reconciliation creates explicit match/evidence records and never rewrites posted `SupplierPayment`, customer `Payment`, `Refund`, `JournalEntry`, or export history.
- Any accounting impact from treasury workflows must use finance posting services and balanced journal entries.
- Supplier bank settlement is allowed only through the implemented journal-backed full-payment workflow from matched reconciliation evidence. It is not a bank API call, status-only edit, or journal rewrite.
- Returned or failed transfers require bank evidence and formal posting or reversal; they cannot be represented by status-only edits on posted supplier payments.
- Supplier overpayment and advance payment remain blocked until a dedicated account role, lifecycle, and settlement policy are designed.
- Bank, statement, transaction, transfer, match, accounting, import, and remote ids use `ExternalReference`.
- Remittance documents and bank evidence use `DocumentRecord`; they do not replace accounting postings.
- Credentials, access tokens, refresh tokens, private keys, connection strings, bank secrets, and raw provider payloads must not be stored in metadata, external references, document metadata, audit payloads, logs, tests, or documentation.
- Public WebApi, mobile/member, storefront, customer invoice archive/download, issued invoice snapshots, customer payment/refund flows, and finance export package format remain unchanged.

## Implementation Sequence

1. `Bank/Treasury Foundation Design Slice`
   - Outcome: this document. Bank account ownership, cash clearing, bank statement import, reconciliation, returned transfer handling, duplicate payment handling, external identity, evidence, and compatibility boundaries are locked.
2. `Bank/Treasury Foundation Core Model And Admin Slice`
   - Outcome: implemented. Internal `BankAccount`, `BankStatementImport`, and `BankStatementLine` models exist in Billing with WebAdmin Bank Accounts and Bank Statements pages, validation, safe metadata handling, statement totals, deterministic line identity, and event/audit evidence.
   - `FinancialAccount` remains the accounting account and optional bank account linkage is mapping evidence only.
3. `Bank Reconciliation Core Slice`
   - Outcome: implemented. Internal `BankReconciliationMatch` and `BankReconciliationMatchLine` models exist in Billing with WebAdmin list/detail/create/edit/mark matched/cancel, candidate bank statement and posted finance fact selection, unique active statement-line guard, multi-line evidence, totals/difference tracking, review notes, and event/audit evidence.
   - Reconciliation is evidence only and does not mutate supplier payments, customer payments, refunds, journal entries, or export history.
4. `Supplier Payment Bank Settlement Boundary Design`
   - Outcome: completed in [supplier-payment-bank-settlement-boundary-design.md](supplier-payment-bank-settlement-boundary-design.md). Settlement trigger, reconciliation linkage, cash clearing, bank evidence, returned/failed transfer limits, duplicate payment limits, remittance evidence, external ids, WebAdmin limits, public/mobile limits, and bank API credential boundaries are locked.
5. `Supplier Payment Bank Settlement Core Slice`
   - Outcome: completed. Internal evidence-backed settlement exists only for posted supplier payments linked to matched zero-difference reconciliation evidence and a same-business mapped bank Asset account. It debits `CashClearing`, credits the mapped bank account, stores settlement journal/reconciliation linkage, and blocks simple payment reversal after bank settlement.
6. `Returned Transfer And Duplicate Payment Boundary Design`
   - Outcome: completed in [returned-transfer-duplicate-payment-boundary-design.md](returned-transfer-duplicate-payment-boundary-design.md). Returned or failed transfer correction is full-settlement v1 and journal-owned; duplicate bank movement is evidence/attention first; supplier advance/overpayment and bank API adapters remain separate future slices.
7. `Returned Transfer And Duplicate Payment Correction Core Slice`
   - Outcome: implemented. Internal `SupplierPaymentBankCorrection` records correction evidence, posts full-settlement returned-transfer correction through finance posting, and keeps duplicate-payment correction as attention/evidence only.
8. `Supplier Advance And Overpayment Boundary Design`
   - Outcome: completed in [supplier-advance-overpayment-boundary-design.md](supplier-advance-overpayment-boundary-design.md). Supplier advance is a future formal Asset-backed Billing/Finance balance, not negative AP or silent overpayment conversion.
9. `Supplier Advance Core Model And Admin Slice`
   - Outcome: completed. `SupplierAdvance` and `SupplierAdvanceApplication` now provide formal internal/WebAdmin advance creation, posting, cancel-draft, and explicit application to posted supplier invoices through finance posting services.

## Implementation Outcome

The core Bank/Treasury foundation is implemented for internal/WebAdmin use:

- Billing now owns `BankAccount`, `BankStatementImport`, and `BankStatementLine`.
- Bank accounts store operational labels, currency, optional same-business Asset `FinancialAccount` mapping evidence, status, default marker, masked identifiers only, and safe metadata.
- Statement imports store period evidence, header totals, line totals, deterministic normalized line identity, review state, and safe metadata.
- Finance WebAdmin exposes Bank Accounts and Bank Statements with internal create/update/archive/import/cancel flows.
- Finance WebAdmin exposes Bank Reconciliation with internal draft/update/match/cancel flows for matching imported bank statement lines to posted or reversed journal facts.
- Bank statement imports do not create `JournalEntry`, payment mutation, refund mutation, reversal, reconciliation match, supplier advance, overpayment, bank credential, or bank API state.
- Bank reconciliation matches do not create `JournalEntry`, payment mutation, refund mutation, reversal, supplier advance, overpayment, bank credential, bank API state, or finance export format changes.
- Supplier payment bank settlement core is complete. The settlement workflow is internal, evidence-backed by reconciliation, limited to posted supplier payments, journal-backed through `CashClearing` and mapped bank Asset, and unable to rewrite payment, journal, refund, or finance export history.
- Returned/failed transfer and duplicate-payment correction core is complete. Corrections use bank statement and reconciliation evidence, remain full-settlement in v1 for returned transfers, avoid automatic duplicate reversal, and keep bank API adapters as separate future slices.
- Supplier advance core/admin is complete. Advance handling uses a dedicated Asset-backed model and explicit posting/allocation workflows.
- Supplier advance reversal hardening is complete. Posted unapplied advances and active advance applications are reversed only through finance posting and preserve history.
- Public WebApi, mobile/member, storefront, customer invoice archive/download, customer payment/refund flows, and finance export package format remain unchanged.
