# Supplier Payment Bank Settlement Boundary Design

## Summary

This document locks the supplier payment bank settlement boundary and records the internal core implementation outcome. The original boundary step was documentation-only; the implementation later added a small additive schema change and internal/WebAdmin workflow for journal-backed settlement from bank reconciliation evidence. It introduces no bank API integration, credential UI, returned-transfer automation, duplicate-payment automation, supplier advance/overpayment, public/mobile/storefront contract, finance export format change, journal editor shortcut, customer payment/refund flow change, or production bank integration flow.

The core decision is that bank reconciliation evidence can support supplier payment bank settlement, but it does not rewrite `SupplierPayment`, customer `Payment`, `Refund`, `JournalEntry`, or finance export history. Any financial change remains owned by explicit finance posting or reversal handlers.

## Current Darwin Supplier Payment Bank Settlement Findings

- `SupplierPayment` posted and reversed states are authoritative through `JournalEntry` and `JournalEntryLine`.
- `CashClearing` is the current accounting settlement boundary for supplier payment posting and reversal.
- `BankAccount`, `BankStatementImport`, `BankStatementLine`, `BankReconciliationMatch`, and `BankReconciliationMatchLine` exist in Billing.
- Bank reconciliation is evidence-only. It links bank statement lines to posted or reversed finance facts and does not mutate supplier payments, customer payments, refunds, journal entries, or finance export history.
- `ExternalReference`, `DocumentRecord`, `BusinessEvent`, `AuditTrail`, and finance export foundations are available for remote identity, remittance evidence, lifecycle evidence, audit evidence, and outbound accounting export.
- Internal journal-backed supplier payment bank settlement is implemented for posted supplier payments linked to matched reconciliation evidence.
- Returned-transfer automation, duplicate-payment automation, bank API integration, supplier advance, and supplier overpayment are not implemented.
- Public WebApi, mobile/member, storefront checkout, customer invoice archive/download, issued invoice snapshots, customer payment/refund flows, and finance export package format remain unchanged.

## Locked Decisions

- Direct bank settlement is implemented only for `Posted` supplier payments.
- Draft, cancelled, and reversed supplier payments do not receive bank settlement.
- v1 settlement is full-payment and journal-backed: debit `CashClearing`, credit mapped bank Asset account.
- `BankReconciliationMatch` does not mutate supplier payments, customer payments, refunds, journal entries, or finance export history.
- Settlement must link to bank statement reconciliation evidence and cannot be a status-only UI flag.
- Returned and failed transfers require bank evidence plus formal posting or reversal policy; they are not simple payment status edits.
- Duplicate payment handling comes from bank statement identity and reconciliation evidence, not from rewriting payment history.
- Supplier advance and overpayment remain blocked until their own account role, lifecycle, and settlement policy are designed.
- Bank/API credentials, access tokens, refresh tokens, private keys, connection strings, raw bank payloads, and raw provider payloads must not be stored in metadata, document metadata, external references, audit payloads, events, logs, tests, or documentation.
- Finance export package format remains unchanged. If a future settlement posts accounting entries, export consumes those entries through the existing posted `JournalEntry` path.
- Public WebApi, mobile/member, storefront, customer invoice archive/download, issued invoice snapshots, customer payment/refund flows, and finance export package compatibility remain unchanged.

## Implementation Sequence

1. `Supplier Payment Bank Settlement Boundary Design Slice`
   - Outcome: this document. Supplier payment settlement trigger, reconciliation linkage, cash clearing boundary, bank evidence, returned/failed transfer limits, duplicate payment handling, remittance evidence, external ids, export impact, WebAdmin limits, public/mobile limits, and bank API credential boundaries are locked.
2. `Supplier Payment Bank Settlement Core Slice`
   - Outcome: implemented. The workflow adds additive `SupplierPayment` settlement fields, requires matched zero-difference reconciliation evidence, requires a bank account mapped to an Asset `FinancialAccount`, posts debit `CashClearing` and credit mapped bank account through `FinancePostingService`, and exposes an internal WebAdmin settlement action.
3. `Returned Transfer And Duplicate Payment Boundary Design`
   - Outcome: completed in [returned-transfer-duplicate-payment-boundary-design.md](returned-transfer-duplicate-payment-boundary-design.md). Returned/failed transfer correction is full-settlement v1, evidence-backed by bank statement and reconciliation, journal-owned, and separate from supplier payment history rewrite. Duplicate bank movement is evidence/attention first and never auto-reversal.
4. `Returned Transfer And Duplicate Payment Correction Core Slice`
   - Outcome: implemented. `SupplierPaymentBankCorrection` records evidence-backed correction state. Returned-transfer correction posts a full-settlement journal-backed correction and duplicate-payment correction remains attention/evidence only.
5. `Supplier Advance And Overpayment Boundary Design Slice`
   - Design supplier credit/advance ownership, account roles, aging, reconciliation, and posting policy before enabling payment beyond open payable.
6. `Bank API Target Adapter Design Slice`
   - Design only after a real bank target, credential owner, payload mapping, error contract, and smoke strategy exist.

## Implementation Outcome

The core implementation is complete for internal/WebAdmin use:

- `SupplierPayment` stores bank settlement timestamp, settlement journal link, reconciliation match link, and safe notes.
- `JournalEntryPostingKind.SupplierPaymentBankSettled` records the clearing entry.
- Settlement is full-payment only and requires a matched reconciliation whose supplier-payment-linked amount equals the payment total.
- Bank-settled supplier payments cannot be reversed by the existing full-payment reversal handler; returned or failed bank movement uses the formal correction workflow.
- Returned-transfer correction core is complete for internal/WebAdmin use and does not rewrite original supplier payment, journal, reconciliation, or export history.
- Duplicate-payment correction is evidence/attention only and cannot be auto-posted.
- No bank API integration, credential UI, returned-transfer automation, duplicate-payment automation, supplier advance/overpayment, public/mobile/storefront contract, customer payment/refund flow, journal editor behavior, or finance export format changed.
- The next gate is `Supplier Advance And Overpayment Boundary Design Slice`.
